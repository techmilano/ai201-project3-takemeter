# Labeling Notes — TakeMeter UCF Dataset

## File
`data/takemeter_ucf_labeled.csv` — 220 rows, columns: `text`, `label`, `source`, `notes`.

## How the data was produced
The comment texts are **hand-written representative examples** in the style of public
r/ucf and r/UCFstudenthousing discussion. They are **not** scraped verbatim from
specific users' posts (avoids attaching real usernames/permalinks and the privacy and
reliability problems of bulk-scraping comments). Topics mirror the assignment's
recommended threads: housing, parking, dining, roommates, off-campus apartments,
freshman advice, transfer advice, and campus complaints. The CSV is regenerated by
`data/build_dataset.py`.

## Label taxonomy (4 labels, used exactly as-is)
- `practical_advice` — actionable guidance, steps, recommendations, or warnings another student could use.
- `personal_experience` — mainly describes the writer's own experience, no clear recommendation.
- `complaint_or_warning` — negative opinion, frustration, or warning about a place, rule, service, or situation.
- `low_info_reaction` — short emotional reaction, joke, agreement, sarcasm, or low-detail reply.

## Decision rule for the hard boundary
The hardest boundary is `personal_experience` vs `practical_advice`.
- If the comment mainly tells **what happened to the writer** → `personal_experience`.
- If it tells another student **what to do, avoid, check, or consider** → `practical_advice`
  (even when it includes personal context, the main purpose is advice).

`complaint_or_warning` vs `low_info_reaction`: if there is a specific gripe or caution
with at least some substance → `complaint_or_warning`; if it is just a short emotional
quip with no usable detail → `low_info_reaction`.

## Label distribution
| label | count |
|---|---|
| practical_advice | 58 |
| personal_experience | 56 |
| complaint_or_warning | 56 |
| low_info_reaction | 50 |
| **total** | **220** |

Roughly balanced (~50–58 per class) to avoid the model over-predicting a majority class.

## Ordering
Rows are interleaved round-robin across labels so every class is spread across the file,
keeping the 70/15/15 split well-mixed even before the notebook shuffles.

## Notebook usage
Point the Colab notebook at `data/takemeter_ucf_labeled.csv`. Only `text` and `label`
are required for training; `source` and `notes` are documentation columns.
